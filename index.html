Summary

The tournament format expanded over the years, with 24 teams participating in the most recent editions. The competition showcases thrilling matches, fierce rivalries, and extraordinary individual performances. The final match determines the champion of Europe, creating unforgettable moments in football history. Notable teams like Germany, Spain, and Italy have enjoyed success in the Euro, while emerging talents and underdog stories contribute to the tournament's appeal. Euro 2020, held in 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, witnessed Italy emerging as the champions after a dramatic final against England at Wembley Stadium. The Euro remains a cornerstone of international football, uniting fans across the continent in a celebration of skill, passion, and sportsmanship.
